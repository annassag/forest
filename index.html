Prologue

In act two of Shakespeare’s 1599 comedy As You Like It the characters, for differing reasons, all seek refuge in the Forest of Arden. Shakespeare is using the forest as a mode, an impermanent refuge, a place of magical transformation outside the constraints of the real world. Desire flows from one character to the other and time is both suspended and subjective at once.

ROSALIND [dressed as Ganymede]: I pray you, what is ‘t the o’clock?

ORLANDO: You should ask me what time o’day. There’s no clock in the forest.

But time functions in many ways, mobility too. Social groups, and in this instance: the characters of As You Like It, have different and unequal relationships to time and space–some people come and go as they wish, others are dependent on other people’s time and presence, others again have the forest as their mainstay. In this choose-your-own-adventure style text message version of Shakespeare’s play, you walk in and out and around in the forest in the shoes of the chosen character, or if you wish: at their level of time. The Forest of Arden, All-Day. Who is the last to exit?

JAQUES: All the world’s stage,
And all the men and women merely players.
They have their exits and their entrances.
